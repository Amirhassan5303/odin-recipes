How to Make kabab-koobideh Step-By-Step

Persian Kabab Koobideh is a classic Iranian grilled dish made from finely ground meat, traditionally a mix of lamb and beef, combined with grated onion, salt, and pepper. The key to a good koobideh is the texture of the meat and onion: the onion is grated and thoroughly squeezed to remove excess juice, ensuring the mixture binds well and does not fall off the skewers. The meat is kneaded well until it becomes smooth and sticky, which helps it hold together during grilling.

The seasoned meat is shaped by hand onto wide metal skewers and cooked over hot charcoal, where it develops a smoky flavor and a slightly charred exterior while staying juicy inside. During grilling, the kababs are often brushed with melted butter or saffron water for extra aroma. Kabab Koobideh is typically served with basmati rice, grilled tomatoes, raw onions, and herbs, making it one of the most popular and beloved Persian meals.

ingredients

  • Ground meat (lamb, beef, or a mix of both)
  • Onion, grated and well drained
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Optional: sumac (for serving)
  • Optional: butter or saffron water (for brushing while grilling)

Steps

  1. Grate the onion and squeeze it thoroughly to remove all excess juice.
  2. Combine the ground meat, grated onion, salt, and black pepper in a bowl.
  3. Knead the mixture well for several minutes until it becomes smooth and sticky.
  4. Cover the meat and refrigerate it for 30â€“60 minutes to help it firm up.
  5. Divide the mixture and press it evenly onto wide metal skewers, shaping ridges with your fingers.
  6. Grill over hot charcoal, turning as needed, until fully cooked and lightly charred; brush with butter or saffron water if desired.
